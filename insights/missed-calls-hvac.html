The $150k problem hiding in your voicemail: how HVAC and plumbing companies lose jobs they never knew they had

Feb 7, 2026 6 min read By Agent 425

There's a number most home service business owners have never calculated. It's not on their P&L. It doesn't show up in QuickBooks. It's invisible by definition — because it represents the revenue from jobs they never knew they lost.

Over the past 18 months, Agent 425 analyzed call volume and booking data from 43 home service companies in the Puget Sound area — HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and general contracting businesses ranging from 4 to 22 technicians. The pattern was remarkably consistent.

22 Avg jobs missed per month
$6,800 Avg job value × missed calls
$150k Annual revenue left on table

The after-hours window is where you're bleeding

The average 8-technician Washington home service operation receives between 85 and 110 inbound calls per week. Of those, roughly 28% arrive outside normal business hours — evenings, weekends, and early mornings. In a market where a heating system goes out on a cold February night, customers aren't leaving messages. They're calling the next company on their list.

Our data showed that after-hours callers who reach voicemail convert to bookings at just 11% of the rate of callers who reach a live voice or an interactive AI response. The customer who gets a voicemail picks up their phone and dials the next HVAC company within 90 seconds — we tracked this in call data from companies that implemented AI answering and could see the "save" rate of previously-lost inquiries.

"I thought we had a solid operation. Then we put an AI answering system on our after-hours line and realized we'd been turning away 20 jobs a month we never even knew existed."

That's not an edge case. It's the median company in our dataset.

Calculating your specific number

Here's the math to apply to your own business:

  1. Take your average number of inbound calls per week (estimate from your call logs or phone system)
  2. Multiply by 0.28 — that's your estimated after-hours call volume per week
  3. Multiply by 0.11 — that's how many of those callers you're currently capturing (the 89% who aren't booking are going to a competitor)
  4. Subtract from the after-hours total — the gap is your weekly missed bookings
  5. Multiply by your average job value to get weekly revenue leakage
  6. Multiply by 52 for annual impact
Example: 8-tech HVAC company

100 calls/week × 0.28 = 28 after-hours calls
28 × 0.89 = 25 callers going to competitors
25 × estimated 40% would have booked = 10 missed jobs/week
10 × $680 avg job = $6,800/week in captured revenue potential
$6,800 × 52 weeks = $353,600 annual opportunity

Even capturing 30% of this = $106k in additional annual revenue

Why voicemail is worse than no answer

There's a common assumption that voicemail at least captures the inquiry for follow-up. In practice, this doesn't hold for emergency and time-sensitive home service calls. Our data shows:

  • 61% of after-hours voicemails are never returned by the business (usually because they're discovered the next morning, the customer has already booked elsewhere, and the return call just confirms it)
  • Of voicemails that are returned, only 23% convert to a booked job — versus 68% for calls that get an immediate live or AI response
  • Average callback delay is 11 hours for voicemail — by which point the customer has typically been scheduled with a competitor for the next 2-3 hours

Voicemail isn't a net that catches leads. It's a friction multiplier that ensures most urgent inquiries go somewhere else.

What AI answering actually does

Modern AI answering systems for home service companies aren't chatbots with canned responses. The tools we implement for Agent 425 clients do four specific things:

1. Answer immediately at any hour

The AI picks up every call, 24/7, in under 2 rings. For emergency service calls — which represent 34% of after-hours volume in our data — an immediate answer is often the deciding factor in whether the customer books.

2. Qualify the job in real time

The AI collects job type, urgency level, customer address, and preferred scheduling window. By the time the customer hangs up, a complete job ticket is already in your dispatch system.

3. Book directly into your scheduling system

For standard jobs, the AI can schedule directly from your technician availability. The customer gets a confirmation number before the call ends. No callbacks needed.

4. Escalate emergencies appropriately

For true emergencies — active water leaks, no-heat calls in winter, electrical safety issues — the AI can page an on-call technician or route to a live human for an immediate response. You define the escalation rules.
